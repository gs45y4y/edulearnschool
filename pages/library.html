Library Services

  • Non-Fiction & Biography

Research Assistance

Our librarians provide expert guidance on research projects, citations, and finding credible sources. Schedule individual appointments or attend drop-in sessions during lunch.

  • Database Tutorials
  • Citation Help (MLA, APA, Chicago)
  • Source Evaluation
  • Research Strategy Development

Online Resources & Databases

Access these premium research databases from school or home using your student login credentials.

EBSCO Research Databases

Comprehensive collection of academic journals, magazines, and newspapers covering all subject areas. Includes full-text articles and citation tools.

Gale In Context

Topic-specific research portal with multimedia content, primary sources, and curriculum-aligned materials for middle and high school students.

ProQuest Historical Newspapers

Access to digitized historical newspapers dating back to the 1800s. Perfect for history research and primary source analysis.

JSTOR

Academic journal archive covering arts, sciences, humanities, and social sciences. Ideal for advanced research projects.

NoodleTools

Online platform for note-taking, outlining, citation management, and collaborative research. Supports MLA, APA, and Chicago styles.

Britannica School

Reliable encyclopedic reference with age-appropriate content levels, multimedia resources, and curriculum tools.

World Book Online

Trusted reference encyclopedia with articles, multimedia, primary sources, and student-friendly interface.

Mango Languages

Learn over 70 world languages through interactive lessons. Perfect for language class support or personal enrichment.

Library Policies

Checkout Policy

Students may check out up to 5 items at a time for a 3-week period. Items can be renewed online or in person unless they have been requested by another student. Late fees are not charged, but students with overdue items may have checkout privileges suspended until materials are returned.

Behavior Expectations

The library is a shared learning space where all students should feel welcome and comfortable. Expected behaviors include:

  • Respect for others working in the library
  • Use appropriate voice levels (quiet zone vs. collaborative areas)
  • Keep the space clean and organized
  • Use technology responsibly and for educational purposes
  • Follow staff directions

Lost or Damaged Materials

Students are responsible for materials checked out on their account. Lost items must be paid for at replacement cost. Damaged items will be assessed on a case-by-case basis. Payment arrangements can be made through the library office.

Technology Use Policy

Library computers and technology are for educational purposes. Students must follow the district's Acceptable Use Policy. Inappropriate use may result in loss of technology privileges. Social media, gaming, and streaming are not permitted unless part of an approved class assignment.

Food and Drink

Water bottles with secure lids are permitted in the library. No food or other beverages are allowed to protect library materials and equipment.
