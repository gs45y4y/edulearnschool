Attendance Policy

Regular attendance is essential for academic success. Colorado law requires students to attend school regularly. Parents must call the attendance line at (719) 384-7255 before 9:00 AM to report absences. Students who accumulate excessive absences may face academic penalties and truancy procedures. For excused absences, students have the number of days absent plus one day to make up missed work. Please refer to the student handbook for complete attendance policies.

Important Policies

Grading Policy

EduLearn K-12 uses a standard letter grade system with the following scale:

  • A: 90-100% (4.0 GPA)
  • B: 80-89% (3.0 GPA)
  • C: 70-79% (2.0 GPA)
  • D: 60-69% (1.0 GPA)
  • F: Below 60% (0.0 GPA)

AP and Honors courses receive weighted grades for GPA calculation. Progress reports are issued mid-semester, and report cards are distributed at the end of each semester.

Dress Code

Students are expected to dress in a manner that is appropriate for a learning environment. Specific guidelines include:

  • Clothing must cover from shoulder to mid-thigh
  • No clothing with offensive language, images, or references to drugs, alcohol, or violence
  • Shoes must be worn at all times
  • Hats and hoods may be worn but faces must be visible
  • Clothing must not disrupt the educational environment

Cell Phone and Electronic Device Policy

Students may possess cell phones and personal electronic devices on campus. However, devices must be silenced during class time unless the teacher gives permission for educational use. Unauthorized use during class may result in confiscation. The school is not responsible for lost, stolen, or damaged personal devices.

Academic Integrity

EduLearn K-12 maintains high standards of academic honesty. Plagiarism, cheating, and unauthorized collaboration are serious violations that may result in a zero on the assignment, parent notification, and possible suspension. Students are expected to complete their own work and properly cite all sources.

Student Services

Counseling Services

Our counseling team provides academic guidance, college and career planning, and personal support. Students are assigned a counselor based on last name. Walk-in hours are available daily from 7:30-8:00 AM and 3:15-4:00 PM. Appointments can be scheduled through the counseling office.

Health Services

The school nurse is available during regular school hours for illness, injury, and medication management. Students must have current immunization records on file. Emergency contact information must be kept up to date in the school system.

Library Services

The library is open Monday through Friday from 7:30 AM to 4:00 PM. Students have access to over 15,000 books, online databases, research assistance, and computer workstations. Study rooms are available for reservation.

Food Services

Breakfast is served from 7:30-7:55 AM. Lunch options include hot meals, salad bar, and grab-and-go items. Free and reduced-price meal applications are available in the main office. Meal accounts can be managed online through the district portal.

Emergency Procedures

In case of emergency, the school follows established safety protocols including lockdown, earthquake, and fire procedures. Parents will be notified via phone, email, and text through our emergency notification system. Please ensure your contact information is current in the student information system. During an emergency, do not call the school as phone lines must remain open for emergency services. Check the school website and social media for updates.
